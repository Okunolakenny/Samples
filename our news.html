Recent Developments in Gift Card Issues

Gift Card Draining Concerns In 2023, Americans lost approximately $217 million due to gift card draining, a scam where thieves steal or alter card numbers to siphon off funds before the legitimate owner can use them. The Federal Trade Commission reported over 41,000 cases of such fraud.

Task Force Initiatives
The U.S. Department of Homeland Security has launched a task force to combat gift card fraud. This initiative aims to address the growing concern of card draining, which affects various retailers, not just one specific brand. Maryland's Legislative Action Maryland has become the first state to pass a law specifically targeting gift card draining.

The Gift Card Scams Prevention Act of 2024 mandates:
Secure packaging for gift cards sold in stores to prevent unauthorized access to card numbers.
Online sellers must register gift cards with the Office of the Attorney General's Division of Consumer Protection. Training for employees on detecting gift card fraud.

Consumer Tips to Avoid Gift Card Fraud
Opt for Virtual Cards Consider giving virtual gift cards instead of physical ones to eliminate the risk of tampering.
Purchase from Trusted Locations If buying physical cards, choose those located near the register or behind the counter to reduce the chance of tampering.

Immediate Registration and Balance Check
Recipients should check the balance and register the card immediately after receiving it to minimize risks.

Report Fraud Promptly
If you suspect fraud, contact the card issuer immediately and report the incident to the Federal Trade Commission.

Conclusion
Gift card scams are a significant issue affecting consumers and retailers alike. With new laws and task forces being established, there is hope for better protection against these fraudulent activities.
Consumers are encouraged to take proactive measures to safeguard their gift card purchases.
